Vietnamese Phò

4 servings

Ingredients

  • 4 pounds beef soup bones (shank and knee)
  • 1 medium onion, unpeeled and cut in half
  • 5 slices fresh ginger
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 2 pods star anise
  • 2 ½ tablespoons fish sauce
  • 4 quarts water
  • 1 (8 ounce) package dried rice noodles
  • 1 ½ pounds beef top sirloin, thinly sliced
  • ½ cup chopped cilantro
  • 1 tablespoon chopped green onion
  • 1 ½ cups bean sprouts
  • 1 bunch Thai basil
  • 1 medium lime, cut into 4 wedges
  • ¼ cup hoisin sauce (Optional)
  • ¼ cup chile-garlic sauce (such as Sriracha) (Optional)

Directions

  1. Preheat the oven to 425°F (220°C).
  2. Place beef bones on a baking sheet and roast until browned, about 1 hour.
  3. Place onion halves on a second baking sheet and roast until blackened and soft, about 45 minutes.
  4. Transfer beef bones and onion halves to a large stockpot. Add ginger, salt, star anise, fish sauce, and water, then bring to a boil. Reduce heat and simmer for 6 to 10 hours.
  5. Strain the broth into a saucepan and set aside.
  6. Place rice noodles in a bowl of room-temperature water and soak for 1 hour. Drain.
  7. Bring the reserved broth to a simmer.
  8. Bring a large pot of water to a boil. Cook the noodles for 1 minute, then drain.
  9. Divide noodles among 4 bowls; top with sirloin, cilantro, and green onion.
  10. Ladle hot broth over each bowl and let sit 1 to 2 minutes until the beef is partially cooked.
  11. Serve with bean sprouts, Thai basil, lime wedges, hoisin sauce, and chile-garlic sauce.
